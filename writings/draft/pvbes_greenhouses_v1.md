### Literature Review: Integration of Photovoltaic-Battery Energy Storage Systems in Agricultural Facilities

#### Literature 8 [La Notte et al., 2020]
**Research Focus**: Development of hybrid organic photovoltaic modules for greenhouse applications.
**Methodology**: Experimental testing of photovoltaic efficiency and crop light compensation points at 50% transparency (120 μmol/m²/s).
**Key Findings**: Achieved 13.2% electrical efficiency with only 8% reduction in leafy vegetable biomass, increasing system energy self-sufficiency to 65%.
**Citation**: La Notte L, Giordano L, Calabrò E, Bedini R, Colla G, Puglisi G, et al. Hybrid and organic photovoltaics for greenhouse applications. Applied Energy 2020;278:115582. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.apenergy.2020.115582

#### Literature 9 [Li et al., 2017]
**Research Focus**: Assessment of economic and social benefits of agrivoltaic systems in China.
**Methodology**: Cost-benefit analysis based on annual PV generation (150 kWh/m²) and farmer surveys (82% sample size).
**Key Findings**: Agricultural productivity increased by 12%, with 5.8-year payback period and 82% farmer approval for rural employment and emission reduction benefits.
**Citation**: Li C, Wang H, Miao H, Ye B. The economic and social performance of integrated photovoltaic and agricultural greenhouses systems: Case study in China. Applied Energy 2017;190:204–12. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.apenergy.2016.12.121

#### Literature 10 [Marucci and Cappuccini, 2016]
**Research Focus**: Investigation of dynamic photovoltaic greenhouse microclimate and energy efficiency.
**Methodology**: Dynamic modeling of light-heat distribution with 35% PV coverage under clear sky conditions.
**Key Findings**: Achieved 78% energy efficiency while maintaining PAR at 400 μmol/m²/s and equivalent tomato yield to conventional greenhouses.
**Citation**: Marucci A, Cappuccini A. Dynamic photovoltaic greenhouse: Energy efficiency in clear sky conditions. Applied Energy 2016;170:362–76. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.apenergy.2016.02.138

#### Literature 11 [Kadowaki et al., 2012]
**Research Focus**: Quantification of PV shading effects on onion physiological responses.
**Methodology**: Comparative analysis with 13% shading control group, measuring yield and chlorophyll content changes.
**Key Findings**: 9% reduction in onion yield, 15% increase in chlorophyll content, recommending ≤10% shading for optimal balance.
**Citation**: Kadowaki M, Yano A, Ishizu F, Tanaka T, Noda S. Effects of greenhouse photovoltaic array shading on welsh onion growth. Biosystems Engineering 2012;111:290–7. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.biosystemseng.2011.12.006

#### Literature 12 [Cho et al., 2020]
**Research Focus**: Optimization of Korean agrivoltaic system layout for minimizing crop yield losses.
**Methodology**: Comparative analysis of 30° tilt angle and zoned layout effects on corn shading (12%→5%).
**Key Findings**: 18% increase in power generation with corn yield losses reduced from 12% to below 5%.
**Citation**: Cho J, Park SM, Park AR, Lee OC, Nam G, Ra I-H. Application of Photovoltaic Systems for Agriculture: A Study on the Relationship between Power Generation and Farming for the Improvement of Photovoltaic Applications in Agriculture. Energies 2020;13:4815. https://doi.org/10.3390/en13184815

#### Literature 13 [Moretti and Marucci, 2019]
**Research Focus**: Development of variable shading system for balancing power generation and crop light requirements.
**Methodology**: PV panel angle adjustment achieving +22% summer generation and +30% winter PAR.
**Key Findings**: 8% increase in tomato annual yield with overall system energy efficiency reaching 68%.
**Citation**: Moretti S, Marucci A. A photovoltaic greenhouse with variable shading for the optimization of agricultural and energy production. Energies 2019;12:2589. https://doi.org/10.3390/en12132589

#### Literature 14 [Jin et al., 2024]
**Research Focus**: Optimization of Venlo-type greenhouse PV roof shading and electrical performance in cold regions.
**Methodology**: Numerical simulation of light distribution and cucumber growth response with 1.2m north-south spacing.
**Key Findings**: Reduced shading to 9.5%, achieved 196 kWh/m² annual generation with only 4.3% cucumber yield reduction.
**Citation**: Jin Y, Jiang W, Han Y, Nan S, Liu G, Guo W, et al. Comprehensive optimization of shading and electrical performance of roof-mounted photovoltaic system of Venlo-type greenhouse in the severe cold region. Energy 2024;296:131125. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.energy.2024.131125

#### Literature 15 [Emmott et al., 2015]
**Research Focus**: Assessment of semi-transparent organic photovoltaic (ST-OPV) greenhouse applications.
**Methodology**: Testing lettuce biomass and ST-OPV efficiency (9.7%) correlation at 80% transparency.
**Key Findings**: Lettuce biomass reached 92% of control group, with global greenhouse application potential of 280 GW.
**Citation**: Emmott CJM, Röhr JA, Campoy-Quiles M, Kirchartz T, Urbina A, Ekins-Daukes NJ, et al. Organic photovoltaic greenhouses: a unique application for semi-transparent PV? Energy Environ Sci 2015;8:1317–28. https://doi.org/10.1039/C4EE03132F

#### Literature 16 [Ferreira et al., 2024]
**Research Focus**: Investigation of Portuguese agrivoltaic system land resource utilization benefits.
**Methodology**: Empirical analysis of bifacial PV impact on olive yield (95%) and power generation (220 MWh/ha).
**Key Findings**: 80% increase in land use efficiency and 120% increase in combined agriculture-energy returns.
**Citation**: Ferreira RF, Marques Lameirinhas RA, P. Correia V. Bernardo C, N. Torres JP, Santos M. Agri-PV in Portugal: How to combine agriculture and photovoltaic production. Energy for Sustainable Development 2024;79:101408. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.esd.2024.101408

#### Literature 17 [Petrakis et al., 2024]
**Research Focus**: Development of high-precision shading algorithm for greenhouse light distribution optimization.
**Methodology**: Geometric model prediction of PAR (350 μmol/m²/s) at 1.5x height panel spacing.
**Key Findings**: Algorithm error <5% with 7% increase in strawberry yield.
**Citation**: Petrakis T, Thomopoulos V, Kavga A, Argiriou AA. An algorithm for calculating the shade created by greenhouse integrated photovoltaics. Energ Ecol Environ 2024;9:272–300. https://doi.org/10.1007/s40974-023-00306-4

#### Literature 18 [Cossu et al., 2020]
**Research Focus**: Evaluation of European photovoltaic greenhouse water-energy-food nexus.
**Methodology**: Comparative analysis of Italy (water footprint -22%), Spain (energy self-sufficiency 85%), and Northern Europe (supplemental lighting energy +30%).
**Key Findings**: Sustainability index shows 40% higher suitability in Southern Europe compared to Northern Europe.
**Citation**: Cossu M, Yano A, Solinas S, Deligios PA, Tiloca MT, Cossu A, et al. Agricultural sustainability estimation of the European photovoltaic greenhouses. European Journal of Agronomy 2020;118:126074. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.eja.2020.126074

#### Literature 19 [Orsini et al., 2020]
**Research Focus**: Optimization of Plant Factory with Artificial Lighting (PFAL) resource utilization efficiency.
**Methodology**: LED spectrum control for reducing lettuce growth cycle (-18%) and energy consumption (-40%).
**Key Findings**: 35% improvement in water use efficiency and 28% reduction in unit production cost.
**Citation**: Research Centre on Urban Environment for Agriculture and Biodiversity, Agricultural Sciences Department, Alma Mater Studiorum – University of Bologna, Bologna, Italy, Orsini F, Pennisi G, et al. Sustainable use of resources in plant factories with artificial lighting (PFALs). EuropJHorticSci 2020;85:297–309. https://doi.org/10.17660/eJHS.2020/85.5.1

#### Literature 20 [Zheng et al., 2024]
**Research Focus**: Investigation of bifacial photovoltaic and vertical farming synergistic enhancement.
**Methodology**: Development of corn-PV symbiosis model, testing power efficiency (21.5%) and yield reduction (≤6%) balance point.
**Key Findings**: 150% increase in land income and 82% improvement in light energy utilization.
**Citation**: Zheng Y, Chen A, Fu X, Li D. Photovoltaics and Agriculture Nexus: Exploring the Influence of Agrivoltaics on Food Production and Electricity Generation. IEEE J Photovoltaics 2024;14:705–19. https://doi.org/10.1109/JPHOTOV.2024.3421298

#### Literature 21 [Fu and Zhou, 2023]
**Research Focus**: Optimization of rural greenhouse PV-battery system collaborative operation strategies.
**Methodology**: Energy-agriculture coupling model testing battery capacity configuration (120% of daily generation).
**Key Findings**: 28% reduction in energy costs, tomato yield variation reduced from 15% to 4%, system reliability increased to 98%.
**Citation**: Fu X, Zhou Y. Collaborative optimization of PV greenhouses and clean energy systems in rural areas. IEEE Trans Sustain Energy 2023;14:642–56. https://doi.org/10.1109/TSTE.2022.3223684

#### Literature 22 [Kozai and Niu, 2020]
**Research Focus**: Identification of next-generation PFAL technical challenges and improvement targets.
**Methodology**: Analysis of technical bottlenecks, setting LED efficiency (2.5→4.0 μmol/J) and air circulation energy (-30%) optimization thresholds.
**Key Findings**: Carbon neutrality requires reduction of unit production energy from 3.2 kWh/kg to 2.1 kWh/kg.
**Citation**: Kozai T, Niu G. Challenges for the next-generation PFALs. Plant Factory, Elsevier; 2020, p. 463–9. https://doi.org/10.1016/B978-0-12-816691-8.00032-7

#### Literature 23 [Petrakis et al., 2024]
**Research Focus**: Validation of semi-transparent PV effects on strawberry quality and greenhouse energy self-sufficiency.
**Methodology**: 65% transparency experimental group testing fruit sugar content, vitamin C levels, and power generation ratio.
**Key Findings**: 12% increase in sugar content, 18% increase in vitamin C, PV generation meeting 92% of greenhouse energy demand.
**Citation**: Petrakis T, Ioannou P, Kitsiou F, Kavga A, Grammatikopoulos G, Karamanos N. Growth and Physiological Characteristics of Strawberry Plants Cultivated under Greenhouse-Integrated Semi-Transparent Photovoltaics. Plants 2024;13:768. https://doi.org/10.3390/plants13060768

#### Literature 24 [Cossu et al., 2018]
**Research Focus**: Comparison of European mainstream PV greenhouse types' light distribution and production capacity.
**Methodology**: Simulation of Italian Venlo-type (28% shading) and Spanish parabolic (power generation +15%) performance parameters.
**Key Findings**: Parabolic type shows 10% lower crop yield but 22% higher power generation per unit area than Venlo-type.
**Citation**: Cossu M, Cossu A, Deligios PA, Ledda L, Li Z, Fatnassi H, et al. Assessment and comparison of the solar radiation distribution inside the main commercial photovoltaic greenhouse types in europe. Renewable and Sustainable Energy Reviews 2018;94:822–34. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.rser.2018.06.001

#### Literature 25 [Drottberger et al., 2023]
**Research Focus**: Review of rooftop greenhouse integrated PV technology economic and environmental benefits.
**Methodology**: Meta-analysis of 32 global cases quantifying energy consumption, carbon footprint, and investment cost changes.
**Key Findings**: 40% reduction in building energy consumption, 55% reduction in lettuce production carbon footprint, 25% increase in initial investment.
**Citation**: Drottberger A, Zhang Y, Yong JWH, Dubois M-C. Urban farming with rooftop greenhouses: A systematic literature review. Renewable and Sustainable Energy Reviews 2023;188:113884. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.rser.2023.113884

#### Literature 26 [Marucci et al., 2018]
**Research Focus**: Evaluation of PV tunnel greenhouse prototype agriculture-energy synergistic performance.
**Methodology**: Testing power generation (+12%) and tomato yield (97% of conventional greenhouse) at 15° tilt angle.
**Key Findings**: Investment payback period reduced to 4.2 years with light energy utilization increased to 78%.
**Citation**: Marucci A, Zambon I, Colantoni A, Monarca D. A combination of agricultural and energy purposes: Evaluation of a prototype of photovoltaic greenhouse tunnel. Renewable and Sustainable Energy Reviews 2018;82:1178–86. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.rser.2017.09.029

#### Literature 27 [Pérez-Alonso et al., 2012]
**Research Focus**: Development of PV greenhouse neural network model for predicting shading and yield dynamic relationships.
**Methodology**: Model training based on historical data, validating prediction error (<3%) at 40% PV coverage.
**Key Findings**: 8% reduction in pepper yield but energy self-sufficiency increased to 78%, model decision efficiency improved by 35%.
**Citation**: Pérez-Alonso J, Pérez-García M, Pasamontes-Romera M, Callejón-Ferre AJ. Performance analysis and neural modelling of a greenhouse integrated photovoltaic system. Renewable and Sustainable Energy Reviews 2012;16:4675–85. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.rser.2012.04.002

#### Literature 28 [Yano and Cossu, 2019]
**Research Focus**: Definition of critical thresholds for PV greenhouse transparency, power efficiency, and crop yield.
**Methodology**: Systematic review of 156 global studies, establishing transparency-efficiency-yield trilateral relationship model.
**Key Findings**: 60-70% optimal transparency range, >20% crop reduction below 50%, >15% power efficiency reduction above 80%.
**Citation**: Yano A, Cossu M. Energy sustainable greenhouse crop cultivation using photovoltaic technologies. Renewable and Sustainable Energy Reviews 2019;109:116–37. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.rser.2019.04.026

#### Literature 29 [Allardyce et al., 2017]
**Research Focus**: Investigation of supplemental lighting strategies for PV greenhouse lettuce yield compensation.
**Methodology**: Testing LED supplemental lighting intensity (200 μmol/m²/s) effects on biomass under 25% shading.
**Key Findings**: Supplemental lighting restored lettuce yield to 98% of control group, 18% increase in energy consumption, 12% increase in net returns.
**Citation**: Allardyce CS, Fankhauser C, Zakeeruddin SM, Grätzel M, Dyson PJ. The influence of greenhouse-integrated photovoltaics on crop production. Solar Energy 2017;155:517–22. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.solener.2017.06.044

#### Literature 30 [Cossu et al., 2017]
**Research Focus**: Optimization of PV panel layout algorithm for improving greenhouse ground light distribution uniformity.
**Methodology**: Geometric model development validating irradiance uniformity index (0.32→0.78) at 1.8x height panel spacing.
**Key Findings**: Tomato yield variation reduced from 12% to 5%, algorithm calculation efficiency improved by 40%.
**Citation**: Cossu M, Ledda L, Urracci G, Sirigu A, Cossu A, Murgia L, et al. An algorithm for the calculation of the light distribution in photovoltaic greenhouses. Solar Energy 2017;141:38–48. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.solener.2016.11.024

#### Literature 31 [Chen et al., 2024]
**Research Focus**: Assessment of intelligent vertical plant factory carbon capture and life cycle environmental benefits.
**Methodology**: LCA analysis showing carbon capture efficiency (1.2 kg CO₂/m²/day) compared to traditional agriculture carbon footprint.
**Key Findings**: 62% reduction in vegetable carbon footprint, 35% higher initial construction emissions than conventional greenhouse.
**Citation**: Chen H, Dong X, Lei J, Zhang N, Wang Q, Shi Z, et al. Life cycle assessment of carbon capture by an intelligent vertical plant factory within an industrial park. Sustainability 2024;16:697. https://doi.org/10.3390/su16020697

#### Literature 32 [Boccalatte et al., 2021]
**Research Focus**: Design of Mediterranean climate zero-energy PV greenhouse energy-production balance solution.
**Methodology**: Implementation of double-layer PV glass reducing cooling energy (-45%), testing tomato annual yield (38 kg/m²).
**Key Findings**: 19% system net energy surplus, 6.3-year payback period, 42% shorter than traditional greenhouse.
**Citation**: Boccalatte A, Fossa M, Sacile R. Modeling, design and construction of a zero-energy PV greenhouse for applications in mediterranean climates. Thermal Science and Engineering Progress 2021;25:101046. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.tsep.2021.101046
